# Cycling restrictions overlay

Renders an overlay that highlights roads where cycling is forbidden or restricted. In a way this is meant to be the antagonist to maps like [CyclOSM](https://www.cyclosm.org/) and [OpenCycleMap](https://www.opencyclemap.org/) that have a focus on rendering bicycle infrastructure. Bicycle infrastructure is almost always built _against_ bicycles rather than _for_ bicycles, meaning that its primary purpose is to make car traffic faster and more comfortable by getting cyclists off the road. Bicycle infrastructure is usually designed by people who never actually cycle, making it full of obstacles that make cycling slow, uncomfortable and dangerous. It is mostly built and maintained with the barest minimum financial effort that can just about justify asking anyone to use it. Bicycle infrastructure can feel beneficial for cyclists when one considers the risks that fast motorized traffic poses towards cyclists (the risk of accidents and the risk of violence) as an unavoidable law of nature, rather than the consequence of prioritizing speed and comfort of car drivers over the well-being of cyclists and the environment, and a culture of violence against cyclists with a legal system that tolerates or even encourages it. Designated bicycle infrastructure is almost always worse than general traffic infrastructure (if cars were absent or at least not constantly endangering other traffic participants, no one would use bicycle infrastructure). This map aims to give those cyclists who are experienced and/or brave enough to use the general traffic infrastructure an idea which roads they are allowed to use and where cycling might be tolerated, as best as this information can be inferred from the data available on OpenStreetMap.

The overlay tiles have a transparent background and are designed to be shown on top of [openstreetmap-carto](https://github.com/openstreetmap-carto/openstreetmap-carto) tiles. The overlay road widths match those of OSM-Carto. Directional arrows are configured to align well with one-way street arrows from OSM-Carto.

Three types of objects are rendered in the overlay:
* Ways with a [`highway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:highway) tag and cycling restrictions are entirely coloured according to their specific type of restriction.
* Ways with a [`highway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:highway) tag that have directional cycling restrictions (restrictions in one direction or different restrictions in both directions) get arrows in a colour representing the type of restriction. Arrows may appear in combination with the road being coloured, if there are directional restrictions in combination with general restrictions.
* [Highway areas](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:area#Highway_areas) (ways or relations with a [`highway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:highway) tag and [`area=yes`]([`highway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:area%3Dyes), in particular pedestrian areas) with cycling restrictions are entirely coloured according to the type of restriction.

There are various tags in OpenStreetMap that indicate cycling restrictions, but their meaning varies greatly across different regions. From the tags we cannot infer whether cycling is allowed, tolerated or desirable on a specific road. Because of this, the overlay highlights the different types of restrictions in different colours, so that a user with knowledge of the local legislation and culture and their own preferences can make an informed decision whether a road is suitable.

Ways and areas may be coloured with the following colours:
* Red (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/D32F2F/D32F2F.png" valign="middle"> `#D32F2F`): Any roads where cycling is explicitly forbidden ([`bicycle=no`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dno), [`bicycle=dismount`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddismount) or [`vehicle=no`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:vehicle%3Dno)), as well as footways ([`highway=footway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dfootway)). Pedestrian streets ([`highway=pedestrian`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dpedestrian)) are not shown if they have no explicit bicycle permissions, as their regulations vary across different regions.
* Dark blue (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/4B0082/4B0082.png" valign="middle"> `#4B0082`): Motorways ([`highway=motorway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dmotorway) and [`highway=motorway_link`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dmotorway_link)) where cycling is not _explicitly_ allowed or forbidden. Whether it is allowed and/or accepted here depends on the specific local conditions.
* Purple (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/8B008B/8B008B.png" valign="middle"> `#8B008B`): Motorroads ([`motorroad=yes`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:motorroad%3Dyes)) where cycling is not _explicitly_ allowed or forbidden. Cycling is _implicitly_ forbidden, but in some places it might be tolerated.
* Orange (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FF6600/FF6600.png" valign="middle"> `#FF6600`): Any roads where cycling is not _explicitly_ allowed or forbidden, but it is _implicitly_ forbidden because there is a compulsory parallel cyclepath ([`bicycle=use_sidepath`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Duse_sidepath)). Cycling might be allowed under certain circumstances, for example when training on a racing bike or if the cyclepath is inaccessible due to snow. Note that the data on OpenStreetMap is very incomplete in this regard and the tag is missing on many roads. The signage can also be legally ambiguous in many places, meaning that it is often not clear whether a bike path is actually compulsory or not.
* Lime (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/AEEA00/AEEA00.png" valign="middle"> `#AEEA00`): A separate bicycle path exists along this road and is optional to use ([`bicycle=optional_sidepath`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Doptional_sidepath)). Cyclists are allowed to use the road, but depending on the local culture, there is a risk of getting assaulted or attacked, especially by car drivers. Note that the data on OpenStreetMap is very incomplete in this regard and the tag is missing on many roads.
* Teal (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/00695C/00695C.png" valign="middle"> `#00695C`): Footpaths or pedestrian zones ([`highway=footway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dfootway), [`highway=pedestrian`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dpedestrian), [`foot=designated`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:foot%3Ddesignated)) where cycling is explicitly allowed ([`bicycle=yes`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dyes), [`bicycle=discouraged`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddiscouraged), [`bicycle=permissive`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dpermissive)), and living streets ([`highway=living_street`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dliving_street)). Cycling is allowed here, but pedestrians have priority. In some places there might be an implicit speed limit, such as walking speed. Roads with [`bicycle=designated`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddesignated) are not shown, since cycling is allowed without restrictions there.

Ways may get the following types of arrows:
* Orange (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FFFFFF/FF6600.png?text=%E2%86%92" valign="middle"> `#FF6600`): Cycling in this direction is implicitly forbidden because there is a parallel compulsory bike path (`bicycle:forward=use_sidepath` or `bicycle:backward=use_sidepath`).
* Lime (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FFFFFF/AEEA00.png?text=%E2%86%92" valign="middle"> `#AEEA00`): There is a parallel optional bike path in this direction (`bicycle:forward=optional_sidepath` or `bicycle:backward=optional_sidepath`).
* Teal (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FFFFFF/00695C.png?text=%E2%86%92" valign="middle"> `#00695C`): Footpaths or pedestrian zones where cycling is explicitly allowed in this direction (`bicycle:forward` or `bicycle:backward` is `yes`, `discouraged`, `permissive`)
* Green (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FFFFFF/00C853.png?text=%E2%86%92" valign="middle"> `#00C853`): Cycling is allowed in this direction on a road where you might not expect it. This applies to oneway streets ([`oneway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:oneway)) where cycling is allowed in both directions ([`oneway:bicycle=no`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:oneway:bicycle)) and roads that have one of the directional restrictions above but have no restrictions in the other direction.
* Red arrows are _not_ used to indicate cycling being forbidden in one direction, firstly because the shape of an arrow feels like traffic _can_ flow in its direction, and secondly because all dual carriageways would need such arrows, which would clutter the map. Instead, when cycling is forbidden in one direction, a direction arrow in the other direction is always rendered in its respective colour. This means that streets with arrows in one direction are always one-way streets for cyclists, while streets with no arrows or streets with arrows in both directions are two-way streets. Regular oneway streets ([`oneway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:oneway)) without any specific cycling rules do not get arrows, as they already have arrows on the OSM Carto map.
