Renders an overlay that highlights roads where cycling is forbidden or restricted. The tiles have a transparent background.

The following colours appear on the tiles:
* Red (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/D32F2F/D32F2F.png" valign="middle"> `#D32F2F`): Any roads where cycling is explicitly forbidden ([`bicycle=no`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dno), [`bicycle=dismount`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddismount) or [`vehicle=no`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:vehicle%3Dno)), as well as footways ([`highway=footway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dfootway)). Pedestrian streets ([`highway=pedestrian`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dpedestrian)) are not shown if they have no explicit bicycle permissions, as their regulations vary across different regions.
* Dark blue (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/4B0082/4B0082.png" valign="middle"> `#4B0082`): Motorways ([`highway=motorway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dmotorway) and [`highway=motorway_link`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dmotorway_link)) where cycling is not explicitly allowed or forbidden. Whether it is allowed and/or accepted here depends on the specific local conditions.
* Purple (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/8B008B/8B008B.png" valign="middle"> `#8B008B`): Motorroads ([`motorroad=yes`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:motorroad%3Dyes)) where cycling is not explicitly allowed or forbidden. Cycling is implicitly forbidden, but in some places it might be tolerated.
* Orange (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/FF6600/FF6600.png" valign="middle"> `#FF6600`): Any roads where cycling is not explicitly allowed or forbidden, but it is implicitly forbidden because there is a compulsory parallel cyclepath ([`bicycle=use_sidepath`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Duse_sidepath)). Cycling might be allowed under certain circumstances, for example when training on a racing bike or if the cyclepath is inaccessible due to snow.
* Teal (<img src="https://placehold.co/15x15/00695C/00695C.png" valign="middle"> `#00695C`): Footpaths or pedestrian zones ([`highway=footway`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dfootway), [`highway=pedestrian`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dpedestrian), [`foot=designated`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:foot%3Ddesignated)) where cycling is explicitly allowed ([`bicycle=yes`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dyes), [`bicycle=discouraged`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddiscouraged), [`bicycle=optional_sidepath`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Doptional_sidepath), [`bicycle=permissive`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Dpermissive)), and living streets ([`highway=living_street`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dliving_street)). Cycling is allowed here, but pedestrians have priority. In some places there might be an implicit speed limit, such as walking speed. Roads with [`bicycle=designated`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:bicycle%3Ddesignated) are not shown, since cycling is allowed without restrictions there.
